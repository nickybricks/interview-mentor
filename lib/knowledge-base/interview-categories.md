# Interview Categories

The Interview Mentor app organizes interview preparation around five core categories. Each category targets a distinct dimension of candidate evaluation, ensuring comprehensive preparation.

## 1. Experience

**What interviewers assess:** Your professional track record, relevant accomplishments, and ability to draw on past work to demonstrate capability.

**Common question types:**
- "Walk me through your resume."
- "Tell me about your most significant professional accomplishment."
- "Describe a project where you had the most impact."
- "What experience do you have with [specific technology/domain]?"

**How to prepare:**
- Map your career highlights to the target role's requirements
- Prepare 2-3 detailed stories per major role using the STAR method
- Quantify impact wherever possible (revenue, users, efficiency, team size)
- Connect past experience to the specific challenges of the target role

**Scoring dimensions:** Depth of experience, relevance to role, clarity of contribution, career progression trajectory.

## 2. Problem-Solving

**What interviewers assess:** Your analytical thinking, structured approach to challenges, creativity under constraints, and ability to break down ambiguous problems.

**Common question types:**
- "Tell me about a time you solved a difficult problem."
- "How would you approach [hypothetical scenario]?"
- "Describe a situation where you had to make a decision with incomplete information."
- "What's the hardest bug/issue you've debugged?"

**How to prepare:**
- Practice frameworks: define the problem → break into parts → prioritize → solve → validate
- Have 3-4 stories showcasing different problem types (technical, strategic, interpersonal)
- Show your thought process, not just the outcome
- Include examples where you identified the problem before anyone else

**Scoring dimensions:** Structured thinking, creativity, analytical depth, root cause identification, solution quality.

## 3. Leadership

**What interviewers assess:** Your ability to influence, guide teams, make tough decisions, mentor others, and drive initiatives — regardless of formal title.

**Common question types:**
- "Tell me about a time you led a team through a difficult situation."
- "Describe a time you had to influence without authority."
- "How do you handle underperforming team members?"
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager."

**How to prepare:**
- Prepare stories at different leadership scopes (leading self, leading team, leading cross-functional)
- Include examples of both successes and constructive failures
- Show emotional intelligence: how you read situations, adapted your style, managed conflict
- Demonstrate growth: how your leadership approach has evolved

**Scoring dimensions:** Vision-setting, team motivation, conflict resolution, decision quality, accountability, mentorship.

## 4. Technical

**What interviewers assess:** Your domain-specific knowledge, technical depth, ability to communicate complex concepts, and hands-on problem-solving skills.

**Common question types:**
- "Explain [concept] as you would to a non-technical stakeholder."
- "How would you design [system/feature]?"
- "What are the tradeoffs between [approach A] vs [approach B]?"
- "Describe your experience with [specific technology]."
- Live coding, system design, or whiteboard exercises

**How to prepare:**
- Review fundamentals in your domain (data structures, system design, domain patterns)
- Practice explaining technical concepts at different levels of abstraction
- Prepare system design stories: requirements → architecture → tradeoffs → outcomes
- Stay current on industry trends and emerging technologies in your field

**Scoring dimensions:** Technical depth, breadth of knowledge, communication clarity, practical application, awareness of tradeoffs.

## 5. Motivation

**What interviewers assess:** Your genuine interest in the role and company, cultural alignment, long-term career vision, and what drives you professionally.

**Common question types:**
- "Why are you interested in this role/company?"
- "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
- "What motivates you in your work?"
- "Why are you leaving your current position?"
- "What's your ideal work environment?"

**How to prepare:**
- Research the company thoroughly: mission, products, culture, recent news, competitors
- Articulate a genuine connection between your career goals and the role
- Be honest about what energizes you — interviewers detect rehearsed enthusiasm
- Prepare a narrative that connects your past → present → future aspirations

**Scoring dimensions:** Genuine enthusiasm, company knowledge, cultural fit, career alignment, self-awareness.

---

## Using Categories for Preparation

Each interview typically covers multiple categories. A structured preparation approach:

1. **Identify the mix:** Technical roles emphasize Technical + Problem-Solving. Leadership roles weight Leadership + Experience. Startup roles often prioritize Motivation + Problem-Solving.
2. **Score yourself:** Rate your readiness in each category (1-10). Focus preparation on the lowest scores.
3. **Map stories to categories:** Each STAR story can often serve multiple categories. A story about debugging a production outage covers Problem-Solving, Technical, and potentially Leadership.
4. **Practice with variety:** Don't over-prepare one category. Interviewers switch between categories to get a holistic view.
