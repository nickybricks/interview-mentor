# WHO Interview Method

The WHO interview method is a structured hiring framework developed by Geoff Smart and Randy Street, outlined in their book *Who: The A Method for Hiring*. It is widely used by top companies to systematically evaluate candidates through four distinct interview stages.

## The Four Stages

### 1. Screening Interview
- **Purpose:** Quick filter to eliminate mismatches early
- **Duration:** 15-30 minutes, usually by phone
- **Key Questions:**
  - What are your career goals?
  - What are you really good at professionally?
  - What are you not good at or not interested in doing?
  - Who were your last five bosses, and how will they each rate your performance on a 1-10 scale?
- **Decision:** Pass/fail — does this candidate warrant a deeper dive?

### 2. Topgrading Interview (Chronological Deep-Dive)
- **Purpose:** Walk through the candidate's entire career history in chronological order
- **Duration:** 1.5-3 hours
- **For Each Role, Ask:**
  - What were you hired to do?
  - What accomplishments are you most proud of?
  - What were some low points during that job?
  - Who were the people you worked with? (Boss name, title — what will they say about you?)
  - Why did you leave that job?
- **Key Insight:** The threat of reference checks ("What will your boss say?") encourages honesty
- **Pattern Recognition:** Look for patterns of success, growth, accountability, and self-awareness across roles

### 3. Focused Interview
- **Purpose:** Deep-dive into specific competencies required for the role
- **Duration:** 45-60 minutes per interviewer (multiple interviewers, each covering different competencies)
- **Structure:** Each interviewer owns 1-2 competencies from the scorecard
- **Key Questions (per competency):**
  - Tell me about a time you [demonstrated this competency]
  - What was the situation?
  - What actions did you take?
  - What was the result?
  - How did this experience shape your approach going forward?
- **Scoring:** Rate each competency on the scorecard (A/B/C or numeric scale)

### 4. Reference Interview
- **Purpose:** Verify claims and gather external perspective
- **Duration:** 15-30 minutes per reference
- **Number:** 5-7 references (chosen by the interviewer, not just candidate-provided)
- **Key Questions:**
  - In what context did you work with [candidate]?
  - What were [candidate]'s biggest strengths?
  - What were [candidate]'s biggest areas for improvement back then?
  - How would you rate their overall performance on a 1-10 scale? What about a 1-10 for [specific competency]?
  - [Candidate] mentioned they struggled with [X] — can you tell me more about that?

## The Scorecard

Central to the WHO method is the **scorecard**, which defines:
1. **Mission:** A brief statement of the role's core purpose
2. **Outcomes:** 3-8 specific, measurable objectives the person must accomplish (e.g., "Grow revenue from $5M to $10M within 2 years")
3. **Competencies:** The behavioral and technical traits required (e.g., efficiency, coachability, strategic thinking)

## Interview Preparation Tips (for Candidates)

### For Screening Interviews
- Have a concise career narrative ready (2-3 minutes)
- Know your top 3 strengths and 1-2 genuine development areas
- Be prepared for the "boss rating" question — honesty is key since they may actually call

### For Topgrading Interviews
- Prepare a chronological walkthrough of your career
- For each role: know what you were hired to do, what you achieved, and why you left
- Have specific metrics and accomplishments for each position
- Be ready to name former managers — the interviewer may contact them

### For Focused Interviews
- Identify the role's key competencies (from the job description)
- Prepare 2-3 STAR stories per competency
- Focus on YOUR specific actions and decisions, not the team's
- Quantify results wherever possible

### For Reference Preparation
- Give your references a heads-up and context about the role
- Brief them on the key competencies being evaluated
- Ensure your references span different roles and relationships (boss, peer, direct report)

## The 50-Question Bank (Topgrading Essentials)

The WHO method's topgrading interview draws from a bank of approximately 50 core questions that repeat for each career chapter. These questions are designed to be simple but revealing when asked consistently across all roles:

1. What were you hired to do?
2. What accomplishments are you most proud of?
3. What were some low points?
4. Who did you work with? What was your boss's name and title?
5. What will [boss name] say were your biggest strengths?
6. What will [boss name] say were your areas for improvement?
7. How would [boss name] rate your performance on a 1-10 scale?
8. Why did you leave?

These eight questions, repeated for every role in the candidate's career, form the backbone of the topgrading interview and can surface patterns that single-instance behavioral questions miss.
